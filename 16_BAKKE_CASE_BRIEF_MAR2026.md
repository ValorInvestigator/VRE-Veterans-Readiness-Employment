## LEGAL VIOLATIONS AND PROCEDURAL FAILURES

### 1. Failure to Issue Written Determination
- **Authority:** 38 CFR 21.420; M28R Part IV, Section B, Chapter 2
- **Violation:** Verbal denial issued Feb 24. No written determination as of Mar 11 (15+ days). Appeal clock has not started.

### 2. Failure to Apply Reasonable Doubt Standard
- **Authority:** M28R Part III, Chapter 1; mirrors 38 CFR 3.102
- **Violation:** When any doubt exists as to whether a veteran can complete the program and achieve suitable employment, doubt MUST be resolved in favor of the veteran. Veteran's documentation was complete and unrebutted. Denial was issued without written basis.
- **Note:** VRE adopted the reasonable doubt standard when the VA integrated the eVA system. The standard is binding.

### 3. Failure to Assist with Appeal
- **Authority:** M28R mandatory assistance provisions
- **Violation:** VRC is mandated to advise veteran of appeal rights and assist in the appeal process. McPhearson has neither issued the written determination required to trigger the appeal clock nor responded to the Mar 5 assistance request.
- **Strategic note:** Veteran invoked the assistance mandate by formally requesting help obtaining the written denial FROM the VRC -- who is herself the VRC. This creates a documented procedural obligation that cannot be ignored without further violation.

### 4. False Statements at Intake
- **Authority:** M28R VRC ethics provisions; 38 CFR professional conduct standards
- **Violation:** Two VRE staff members made materially false statements at intake designed to discourage veteran from pursuing entitled benefits:
  - "VRE does not pay for college" -- FALSE. Track 3 explicitly covers educational assistance including college. See 38 USC Chapter 31; M28R.
  - "You risk losing your rating if you pursue VRE" -- FALSE. No such mechanism exists. This is a coercive statement.
- **Evidence:** Audio recording (Oregon one-party consent, ORS 165.540). Being preserved for escalation.

### 5. Predetermined Outcome / Truncated Evaluation
- **Evidence:** 2-hour appointment reduced to 1 hour without notice, scheduled before lunch. Evaluation lasted approximately 12 minutes. Verbal denial issued without written basis. Combined with false intake statements, this establishes a pattern of predetermined outcome.
